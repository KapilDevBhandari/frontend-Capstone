The Nepali Sign Language (NSL) Dataset is a continuously growing collection of images and videos that serve as the foundation for training an AI-powered sign language recognition system. This dataset plays a crucial role in enhancing the accuracy and adaptability of NSL interpretation by providing a diverse range of hand gestures, finger movements, and facial expressions. By learning from this structured dataset, the AI system can recognize and translate sign language with greater precision, making communication more accessible for the deaf and hard-of-hearing community.

The dataset consists of categorized sign gestures, including words, phrases, and sentences, along with metadata and labels that describe each sign's meaning and variations. It also incorporates multiple perspectives and hand movement tracking, allowing the AI to learn from different signing styles and angles. Over time, as more data is added, the system becomes more intelligent and inclusive, improving its ability to recognize natural signing patterns.

This dataset is not only essential for training AI models but also serves as a valuable resource for researchers, educators, and accessibility advocates working on gesture recognition and sign language processing. With ongoing contributions from users, the dataset evolves to capture regional and personal signing differences, ensuring that the AI can accurately interpret signs in real-world scenarios. By expanding this dataset, we can bridge the communication gap between the deaf and hearing communities and drive advancements in sign language recognition technology.
